We are we doing this?!

Full width page

We'll tell you why...

We believe women do not enthusiastically choose careers in tech because the support system is not apparent. While 57% of college, US graduates are female, less than 18% choose careers in the STEM fields. There is no specific reason for this but a society that began as one where the workforce was male-dominated will take some time to become diversified. There is also a stigma of the IT culture that I hope to chang gradually. Information Technologists aren’t all older, socially awkward, Caucasian men nor are they young, game-obsessed, super hackers. This is the common perception of an individual who works in IT and it’s reflected in working teams. A woman will often find herself being the only female on a development team out of maybe 10 other colleagues. This is not a problem, per se, but one that I feel should be better represented through the exposure of like-minded women. Once women feel they have the support necessary to succeed in IT, more will follow suit and choose careers in IT.
